Hi, I'm Venki Ramakrishnan.Ph.D.

Venkatraman Ramakrishnan, known as Venki, was born in Chidambaram, India, to scientist parents; his father was a biochemist, and his mother a psychologist. At a young age, his family settled in Baroda (Vadodara), Gujarat, where his father became head of the Biochemistry Department at the University of Baroda. This transition meant switching from Tamil to Gujarati, and he attended an English-language school, which helped him master English.

At 16, Venki joined the University of Baroda, inspired by the new physics curriculum. He graduated with honors before his parents' sabbatical at the University of Illinois. At 19, he enrolled in Ohio University's graduate program instead of continuing undergraduate studies. He married Vera Rosenberry in 1975 and spent 28 years in the U.S., advancing his scientific career.

Structural Biology

Ramakrishnan specializes in determining the three-dimensional structure of biological molecules using techniques such as X-ray crystallography and cryo-electron microscopy

Ribosome Research

His most notable work has been on the structure of the ribosome, a complex molecular machine made of RNA and proteins that synthesizes proteins based on genetic information.

Translation Mechanism

Ramakrishnan’s research has provided insights into the process of translation, where ribosomes read the genetic code from messenger RNA (mRNA) and build proteins.

Antibiotic Resistance

His work has also been critical for understanding how certain antibiotics interact with ribosomes.
